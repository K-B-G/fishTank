What is a Nano Tank?



Small and fun

A nano tank simply means a small sized tank to house aquatic life. For fresh water this generally refers to tanks 10 gallons and down, though 2.5 is generally the bare minimum; though fish should be in 5 gallons minimum. Nano tanks can be a fun hobby and be a nice center piece in any room.

Common Components

Tank

infoInfo

There a many different styles of tanks you can choose from. You can either get a basic glass or acrylic black rimed tank, all in one tanks with filtration system included, or simplistic edgeless clear cubed tanks.

Filter

infoInfo

There are many different types of filters to chose from that provided different types of filtration such as, biological, mechanical, and chemical. The most simplistic filters are sponge filters that provide biological filtration and some mechanical filtration, and Hang Off the Back (HOB) filters that provide biological, mechanical and sometimes chemical filtration. There are also canister filters, but those are not generally used for nano tanks, as they are more suited for large tanks.

Gravel

infoInfo

Gravel in an aquarium not only provides a means to grounds plants and add style, but also helps aid in building beneficial bacteria. Gravel can be simple natural colored pebbles, large river rocks, colorful coated pebbles, and phosphorous infused gravel to aid in plant grow.

Plants

infoInfo

Live plants add not only beauty to an aquarium, but also have a practical function. They help remove nitrates and ammonia from the water. They also can help aid in the growth or beneficial bacteria. While it may seem difficult to grow plants in an aquarium, there are many species of plants that are easy to grow.

Heater

infoInfo

Many plants and fish, especially tropical ones, require a heater to keep the water warmer in order to live. These are especially needed if you live in a cold environment. Heaters can be as simple as fixed temperature setting all the way to advanced micro controller controlled heating. In general the best ones can self regulate their temperature in the water for an all in one solution.

Lighting

infoInfo

Lighting not only helps illuminate your tank so you can view it better it also helps provide light to keep your plants growing. There are many different types of lighting systems that range in function and price. LED overhead lights are become popular. Research the type of light your plants need in order to maintain a healthy aquarium.

Popular Fish and Plants

Below are some easy plants and fish that work well in a nano tank setup. Most of them will work fine in a 5 gallon and up tank.

Cryptocoryne Beckettii

 info_outline
Cryptocoryne Beckettiiclose

Just like the other plants, Cryptocoryne is very easy to care for and a beautiful addition to your aquarium.They come in a range of colors from bright green, bronze, and reddish green.

Scarlet Gem

 info_outline
Scarlet Gemclose

The scarlet badis is a stunning nano fish that has a body shape strikingly similar to that of dwarf cichlids, although there is no relation. The scarlet gem is a timid fish and very peaceful with other fish of similar size and temperament. Its best to house one male Scarlet Badis per every 5 gallons as they can be aggressive towards each other. Due to their shy and timid nature, care should be taken to ensure that they are properly fed. The scarlet badis makes for a unique addition to a small community tank. Find out more about the Scarlet Gem here!

Amazon Sword

 info_outline
Amazon Swordclose

The Amazon Sword plant has leaves that resemble swords. It can grow to be up to 20 inches tall and is ideal for the back of the aquarium. Often they are planted in groups in the back of the aquarium.

Pea Puffer

 info_outline
Pea Pufferclose

Yes, there is such a thing as freshwater puffer fish! In fact, there are several species of freshwater puffers, but this one is the smallest (and the cutest!) and can be kept in a tank as small as 5 gallons. Pea puffers max out at just over an inch in size and should only be grouped with each other, small catfish, and small gobies such as the bumble bee goby. Despite their very tiny size they can be quite aggressive and do not make suitable tankmates for other small, free-swimming fish and can be difficult to house with larger species as well. Pea puffers can also be aggressive towards each other and care should be taken to house a 1:2 ratio of males to females and no more than 3 puffers should be housed in a 5-gallon tank. Pea puffers will do best in a tank that is heavily planted with plenty of hiding spaces and places for this curious fish to explore. Puffers are a delight to have in aquarium; they are very inquisitive and will often swim up to the glass when you approach the tank. As with all puffer fish it has the ability to inflate its stomach with water or air when threatened, puffing up to two to three times its size.

Water Wisteria

 info_outline
Water Wisteriaclose

Water Wisteria grows fast, quickly adding depth and beauty to your freshwater aquarium. It needs minimal maintenance, which makes it a fuss free plant to have.

Endler's Livebearers

 info_outline
Endler’s Livebearersclose

Endlers livebearers are a fun, hardy, and easy species to keep and stay much smaller than their cousin the guppy (Poecilia reticulata). They are a very active fish and easy breeder for beginners. In the aquarium endlers soon learn that humans mean food, after which anytime they see a human near a tank they will go into a frenzy of begging for food. Be careful when housing endlers with guppies as they will interbreed. Endlers are not very shy and are therefore not as fussy when it comes to hiding places and live plants. They do well in pairs as well as in small groups.

Java Fern

 info_outline
Java Fernclose

Java Fern is very popular since it is low maintenance and very beautiful. It can be used in just about any area of the aquarium.

Tiny Gouramis

 info_outline
Tiny Gouramisclose

The liquorice and sparkling gouramis are the smallest of the gouramis, each maxing out at just an inch and half. Gouramis are members of the labyrinth fishes and have a specialized organ called a labyrinth that allows them to breath air from the surface of the water. These smaller species of gouramis can be difficult to care for. They are native to slow-moving waters such as swamps, pools, and flooded fields. These habitats are rich in nutrients, low in oxygen, and very acidic. Maintaining such soft, acidic water in an aquarium can be done with the addition of driftwood, almond leaves and the use of reverse osmosis water.
